Business Model
Three reinforcing revenue streams
01

Education

Tuition & prep

Dual-qualification courses (aged care, nursing), English & exam prep, skills-assessment support. Paid by students / sponsors, often financed against future earnings.

02

Placement

Success fees

Employer pays a placement fee per successful hire (industry benchmark A$6k–15k; ~A$7k avg). Premium for nurses; volume pricing for aged-care cohorts.

03

Platform

SaaS & services

Employer subscriptions (portal access, analytics, compliance vault), featured/priority access, verification & background-check fees, payment processing.

Each placement feeds the next cohort; verified outcomes raise course demand and employer trust — a compounding flywheel.

Why the model scales

  • Recurring demand. The shortage is structural through 2050 — employers re-order cohort after cohort.
  • Multiple shots on goal. If a candidate doesn’t place, education & platform revenue are already earned.
  • Low marginal cost. Software automates matching, document handling and status tracking.
  • Expandable. Same engine extends to hospitality, construction & agriculture, and to Germany / Japan / Korea / Taiwan.

ViecVui · Global Talent Platform10
Technology
A pragmatic, scalable, compliance-ready stack

Frontend

Next.js / React, TypeScript, Tailwind. Responsive employer portal + candidate PWA (Zalo-friendly for VN users).

Backend & API

Node.js (NestJS) or Django, REST + GraphQL gateway, event bus (Kafka/SNS) for status changes.

Data

PostgreSQL (system of record), Redis cache, object storage for documents, search via OpenSearch.

Cloud & DevOps

AWS/GCP, containers (ECS/Kubernetes), IaC (Terraform), CI/CD, staged environments.

AI / Matching

Suitability scoring & candidate–role matching, document OCR & data extraction, multilingual assistant.

Security & Compliance

SSO/MFA, RBAC, encryption at rest & in transit, audit logging; aligned to Australian Privacy Act & VN PDPD.
